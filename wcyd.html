Countries that Offer Free Schooling

Niger has free schooling, but due to a lack of schools and high poverty rate, many children do not go past primary school.
Here are a list of countries with a free, reliable, prestigious and accessible public school system.
While it may be difficult to relocate, the education of your child should be a priority.

In Africa


Mauritius

Mauritius is a small island nation in East Africa with a population of about 1.265 million. The government offers free education to its citizens from pre-primary to tertiary levels. Schooling is compulsory up to the age of 16.

In 2015, Mauritius had a literacy rate of around 91%,
above the world average.


Seychelles

Seychelles is a tiny island nation with a population of around 96,000. It ranks 43rd globally for the best education system, ahead of countries like Russia, UAE, Ukraine, and Hungary. Education is free from pre-primary to secondary school.

In 2015, Seychelles had a literacy rate of around 95%, 10% above world average.


Tunisia

Tunisia is a North African country with a population of around 11.53 million. The government has focused
on the development of the education system since gaining independance in 1956. They spend over 20% of their budget on education.

In 2015, Tunisia had a literacy rate of around 81%.


South Africa

South Africa is a country on the southern end of Africa, with a population of around 56 million. The government spends over 18% of its budget on education, and as a result South African universities compete globally in academics.

In 2015, South Africa had a literacy rate of around 95%.





































In the World

South Korea

South Korea is an East Asian nation with a population of around 51.47 million. Schooling is free for all elementary and middle school students, and will become free for high school students in 2021. South Korea consistenly ranks within the top 3 for best education systems in the world annually.

In 2008, South Korea had a literacy rate of about 98%.


Japan

Japan is an island country in East Asia with a population of around 127 million. Primary and secondary education are free, and free higher education will be implemented in 2020. School is mandatory from elementary to middle school, but most students move on to high school.

In 2002, Japan had a literacy rate of 99%.

Finland

Finland is a northern European country with a population of around 5.5 million. Education is free from pre-primary to secondary levels of education. Finland is renowed for its forward-thinking education system, in which there is no standardized testing.

In 2000, Finland had a literacy rate of 100%.


Denmark

Denmark is a country in Northern Europe with a population of around 5.6 million. Education is mandatory from
pre-primary to secondary levels. Public education is free of charge and open to all.

In 2003, Denmark had a literacy rate of 99%.

The School Fund

If relocation is impossible, another possible alternative is contacting the School Fund.
The School Fund works with local organizations in sub-saharan Africa to select high performing students who are economically disadvantaged
to go to secondary and tertiary schools, via a scholorship that is crowdfunded by the website.

The organization has funded over 1000 students in its short lifetime. Although they currently do not support students from Niger,
getting in touch with local organizations or contacting The School Fund directly can be the first step in making it happen.
